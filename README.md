# Overview

Artificial intelligence is increasingly becoming the first place people go when health symptoms appear.

People now ask AI systems such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude questions about:

* stress
* anxiety
* sleep problems
* dizziness
* fatigue
* chest discomfort
* lifestyle concerns

Despite this growing behavior, little is understood about how people interpret AI-generated health advice and how these responses influence decision-making.

This independent UX research study investigates how different AI response styles influence:

* urgency assessment
* emotional reactions
* intended actions
* information-seeking behavior
* trust and usefulness

---

# Research Question

> How do different AI response styles influence users' perceptions, information-seeking behavior, and intended actions when seeking health advice?

# Evaluated Response Types

### Response 1 — Generic Response

Neutral and medically cautious.

### Response 2 — Empathetic Response

Emotionally reassuring and supportive.

### Response 3 — Structured Response

Action-oriented guidance with recommendations and monitoring advice.

---

# Key Insight

> Users do not seek health answers from AI.

They seek help deciding:

* Should I worry?
* Can I wait?
* Should I monitor this?
* Do I need professional care?

AI increasingly functions as a triage tool rather than a diagnosis tool.

---

# Major Findings

## AI Is The First Stop

Typical behavior:

Symptoms → AI → Self-management → Doctor

---

## Users Seek Urgency Assessment

Participants primarily wanted to understand:

* Is this serious?
* Can I wait?
* What should I do next?

---

## Questions Are Preferred Over Answers

Participants consistently wanted:

* follow-up questions
* clarification
* contextual understanding

Static responses felt incomplete.

---

## Actionability Builds Confidence

Participants valued:

* monitoring periods
* timeframes
* next steps
* practical recommendations

---

## False Empathy Reduces Trust

Unsupported emotional assumptions reduced credibility.

Participants preferred:

* honesty
* transparency
* clarification

over reassurance.

# Ethical Considerations

AI systems increasingly function as:

* emotional support tools
* symptom interpreters
* urgency advisors

This creates responsibilities around:

* emotional dependence
* delayed care
* false reassurance
* unsafe confidence

---

# Reflection

This study began as an investigation into emotional reassurance and trust.

The findings suggested something different.

Participants cared less about trust than expected.

They cared more about:

* understanding symptoms
* assessing urgency
* deciding next actions

The research evolved from trust calibration to decision-making under uncertainty.

---

# Final Insight

> People do not want AI to replace doctors.

> They want AI to help them decide what to do next.
